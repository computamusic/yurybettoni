# 00 · Overview & North Star

## The problem we're solving

The current site treats Yury Bettoni as a list of ventures. A visitor lands and meets a tennis program, a streetwear label, a foundation, and a coaching service — four front doors, no through-line, and no sense of the person they all belong to. The result feels slow, fragmented, and emotionally flat. Animations move without saying anything. The Foundation — which should be the heart — sits on a side branch. The shop lives under a different name (GoSymba) with no bridge back.

We are not redesigning the surface. We are rebuilding the thing that was missing from the start: **a single, coherent world with one protagonist.**

## The protagonist

Yury Bettoni — born in Africa, raised across continents, trained at Bollettieri/IMG, hitting partner for Mary Pierce's 1997 Italian Open title, coach to top-ranked players, then entrepreneur (Italkraft luxury kitchens, the GoSymba brand) and philanthropist. His mother **Alessandra** put a racquet in his hand; in her memory he built the **Alessandra Bettoni Foundation**, bringing tennis and education to youth in Ethiopia and Tanzania.

One man, one through-line: **discipline turned into legacy.** Everything he does — the game, the giving, the building — is the same instinct expressed three ways.

## The brand promise

> **Yury Bettoni — be the architect of your own destiny.**

The site exists to make a visitor feel that instinct is available to them too: through what he teaches (Business), what he honors (Foundation), and what he proved (Athlete).

## The three pillars

The site has three rooms, but it's one house. Navigation always reads as Yury's world; each pillar is a destination *within* it.

| Pillar | What it is | What the visitor should feel | Where it leads |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The Athlete** | The career, the story, the philosophy, the proof. | *Inspired. This person is the real thing.* | Trust → deeper into the world |
| **The Legacy** (Foundation) | The Alessandra Bettoni Foundation. The emotional center. | *Moved. This matters, and it's bigger than a brand.* | Support / donate |
| **The Business** | Courses, coaching, speaking, events, merch. | *This is for me, and it's worth it.* | Buy / inquire / subscribe |

The Athlete earns the trust. The Legacy earns the heart. The Business earns the living. They reinforce each other in that order — credibility before emotion before commerce.

## Design thesis (set in full in docs 06–07)

The world is **clay-court geometry**: precise white lines, deep warm ground, the velocity of the game, Italian heritage, African earth-light. Confident grotesque type for the athlete and the business; a refined serif reserved for the Foundation's quieter voice. The signature device is **the line** — the court's geometry used as the site's structural system, and a single decisive "baseline" drawn on load. Luxury here means restraint: lots of air, exact spacing, motion only when it carries meaning. *Attract, don't scream.*

## Core goals (every page serves at least one)

1. Sell merchandise
2. Sell courses
3. Increase coaching inquiries
4. Grow email subscribers
5. Promote events
6. Drive Foundation support / donations
7. Elevate Yury's personal brand
8. Honor Alessandra's legacy
9. Become the central hub for everything he does

## Success criteria

**On arrival**, within seconds a visitor understands: who Yury is, why he matters, why they should care, what he stands for, and how to engage.

**On leaving**, they feel inspired, trust the brand, understand the mission, and naturally want to *buy, support, learn, or belong*.

**Measurable proxies** (instrumented from launch): merch + course conversion rate, coaching-inquiry submissions, email-capture rate, event RSVPs, donation conversion, and Core Web Vitals in the green on real iPad-landscape + mobile traffic.

## Glossary

- **Pillar** — one of the three destinations (Athlete / Legacy / Business).
- **The Line** — the court-geometry signature device (see 06–07).
- **Tonal shift** — the Foundation's quieter, serif-forward, paper-grounded treatment within the same brand system.
- **The 3 Y's** — Yury's training triad (mobility, stability, velocity); used as authentic structural content, not decoration.
- **Mocked commerce** — real purchase *UX* with placeholder data; no live payment backend this phase.
