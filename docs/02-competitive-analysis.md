# 02 · Competitive Analysis

**Goal:** understand what world-class sites do, then steal the *principles* — never the surface. For each reference we name the one transferable move and how it applies to Yury.

> Note: this is a pattern study, not a screenshot archive. Where a specific live URL is useful as a reference for the build team, it's named; the team should pull current captures during build, since these sites change.

---

## 1. Elite athletes & athlete documentaries

**References to study:** athlete personal brands and documentary-style sites — the "athlete as protagonist" genre (e.g. the editorial player profiles on nike.com, Formula 1 / Drive-to-Survive-style athlete features, and premium athlete portfolio sites featured on Awwwards).

| Pattern | What it does | Apply to Yury |
|---|---|---|
| **Cinematic hero, single statement** | One image/film + one line establishes the person before any nav choice. | Homepage opens on Yury + one decisive line, not a menu of ventures. |
| **Stats as drama** | A few hard numbers, set huge, carry credibility instantly. | A restrained stat layer in mono type — years on tour, players coached, titles touched — *verified only.* |
| **Chronology as narrative** | A scrollable timeline turns a résumé into a story. | The Athlete pillar's career timeline (real years = authentic numbering). |
| **Quiet authority** | Confidence shown through space and restraint, not loud effects. | Sets our "attract, don't scream" motion budget. |

**Trap to avoid:** the "big number + small label + gradient" hero is the genre's default. We use stats, but the *hero thesis* is the line/court geometry, not a number.

## 2. Luxury lifestyle brands

**References to study:** porsche.com, premium automotive & fashion houses, high-end hospitality.

| Pattern | What it does | Apply to Yury |
|---|---|---|
| **Editorial pacing** | Generous whitespace, slow deliberate reveals, one idea per viewport. | Section rhythm across the whole site; no cramming. |
| **Material honesty** | The product's real materials drive the palette (leather, metal, paint). | Our palette comes from clay, chalk line, court ink, African gold — not arbitrary brand colors. |
| **Configurator-grade interaction** | Interactions feel engineered, weighty, precise. | Hover/scroll micro-interactions feel *built*, with consistent easing. |
| **Restraint as luxury signal** | Luxury never shouts the price or the CTA. | Single, calm CTA per section; commerce that feels like a boutique, not a checkout funnel. |

**Trap to avoid:** luxury minimalism can become cold and generic. Our warmth (earth tones, real photography, the personal story) keeps it human.

---

## 3. Premium coaching & expert businesses

**References to study:** high-ticket coaching/expert sites and modern course platforms (the best of the "personal expert" genre — clean, proof-led, not infomercial).

| Pattern | What it does | Apply to Yury |
|---|---|---|
| **Proof before pitch** | Credentials, results, and testimonials precede any "buy." | Athlete credibility is structurally upstream of Business CTAs. |
| **One clear offer ladder** | Free → low → high (content → course → 1:1 coaching) is legible at a glance. | Business pillar presents a clean ladder: newsletter → course → coaching/speaking. |
| **Specific outcomes** | "You will be able to X" beats "world-class methodology." | Course/coaching copy names concrete outcomes (the 3 Y's, measurable improvement). |
| **Human credibility** | Real face, real voice, video. | Yury on film; first-person voice; no stock. |

**Trap to avoid:** the "salesy" coaching aesthetic (loud testimonials, countdown timers, neon CTAs). We invert it — premium restraint, proof-led.

## 4. Modern editorial experiences

**References to study:** digital magazines, design studios, and Awwwards "Site of the Day" editorial winners.

| Pattern | What it does | Apply to Yury |
|---|---|---|
| **Type as the hero** | Distinctive type pairing carries the personality. | Our grotesque/serif/mono system (doc 06) is a deliberate, ownable voice. |
| **Structural devices with meaning** | Eyebrows, numbering, rules that *encode* something true. | "The line" device = real court geometry; numbering = real years/sets, never decorative 01/02/03. |
| **Scroll as storytelling** | Scroll-linked reveals pace the narrative. | Used sparingly for the Athlete timeline and the Foundation story. |
| **Art-directed media** | Images are composed, captioned, intentional. | Every photo earns its place; one consistent grade. |

**Trap to avoid:** editorial sites can over-animate into showreels. Motion stays subordinate to story (doc 07).

## Cross-cutting principles we adopt

1. **Person first, ventures second.** Every reference that works leads with a human or a thesis, never a navigation grid.
2. **Proof → emotion → commerce**, in that order — the spine of our journey design (doc 04).
3. **Material-honest palette** drawn from the subject's real world.
4. **Restraint is the luxury signal.** One idea per viewport, one CTA per section, motion only with meaning.
5. **Specificity is credibility.** Numbers, dates, outcomes, names — verified.

## How the Foundation reads differently (and why that's on-brand)

Best-in-class nonprofit storytelling (the dignity-first end of the genre — think well-made foundation films, not donation-banner pages) shares the luxury world's restraint but trades confidence for *tenderness*: softer light, slower pace, the beneficiary's dignity centered over the founder's brand. We encode this as the Foundation's **tonal shift** — same grid and spacing, but serif voice, paper ground, warmer light, and impact-led copy. It belongs to Yury's world while clearly being the part that isn't about selling.

## What "winning" looks like vs. these references

We don't need more animation or more pages than these sites — we need their **discipline**: a single protagonist, a material-true identity, ruthless hierarchy, and motion that means something. That's the bar doc 06–07 is built to clear.
